Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from investors, partners, and pilot farmers.

How accurate is AgriSense AI's crop disease diagnosis? expand_more
In our Mekong Delta pilot (Q4 2025), AgriSense achieved 91% diagnostic accuracy across rice, maize, and cassava diseases — validated by independent agronomists from the International Rice Research Institute (IRRI). This accuracy rate is for the top-1 predicted disease; top-3 accuracy is 97%. We continue to improve through active learning from agronomist feedback loops.
Does it work without internet access? expand_more
Yes. AgriSense supports an offline-first mode using Google AI Edge for on-device inference. Farmers can capture photos and receive basic disease identification without connectivity. When the device reconnects, the full Gemini-powered analysis — including Search Grounding, market data, and input dealer locations — syncs automatically. In our pilot, 22% of diagnoses were completed in offline mode.
Which crops and diseases are supported? expand_more
AgriSense currently supports 240+ conditions across 4 major crop categories: rice (blast, sheath blight, BPH, tungro, etc.), maize (fall armyworm, northern leaf blight, stalk rot), cassava (mosaic disease, bacterial blight, mealybug), and sugarcane (red rot, smut, top borer). We are expanding to cover coffee, rubber, and tropical fruit crops by Q3 2026.
What languages and dialects are supported? expand_more
AgriSense supports 14+ regional dialects via Google Cloud Speech-to-Text v2 with custom agricultural vocabulary models. This includes Northern, Central, and Southern Vietnamese; Central and Northeastern Thai; Javanese and Sundanese (Indonesia); Tagalog; and Khmer. Text input is supported in all official ASEAN languages. In our pilot, 68% of farmer interactions were voice-based, confirming that voice-first design is critical for adoption.
How does the revenue model work? expand_more
AgriSense operates a three-tier revenue model: (1) Freemium for farmers — basic disease diagnosis is free, with premium features (seasonal analytics, input marketplace, cooperative dashboards) available via subscription at $1–2/month; (2) B2B API licensing — agri-insurers and input suppliers pay for risk assessment data feeds and targeted product recommendations; (3) B2G contracts — government agricultural departments fund subsidised access programmes for their extension service networks. Our unit economics are strong: cost-per-diagnosis is $0.003 (Gemini API), giving us 99%+ gross margins on premium subscriptions.
How is this different from existing agri-tech apps? expand_more
Most existing agri-tech solutions in SEA are either (a) satellite-based platforms (like Cropin, TrueCrop) that require expensive hardware and serve large commercial farms, or (b) generic chatbots that lack domain expertise and hallucinate treatment advice. AgriSense is the first to combine Gemini's native multimodal capability (image + voice + text in a single API call) with Google Search Grounding (zero hallucination) and deep localization (14+ dialects, local input dealer networks). We target the underserved 85% — smallholder farmers with ≤2ha who only need a smartphone.
What Google Cloud services does AgriSense use? expand_more
AgriSense is built entirely on Google Cloud: Gemini 2.0 Flash (multimodal AI engine), Vertex AI (model management and monitoring), Cloud Speech-to-Text v2 (dialect recognition), Google Search Grounding (factual verification), Google Maps Platform (input dealer geolocation), Cloud Run (serverless compute), Firebase (mobile backend, auth, real-time sync), BigQuery (analytics and data intelligence), and Google AI Edge (offline inference). Estimated monthly Google Cloud spend at 50K MAU: $2,800.
